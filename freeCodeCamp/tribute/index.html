Ralph Vaughan Williams

The man who wrote masterpieces

Here's a timeline of Ralph Vaughan Williams life:

  • 1872 - Born in Gloucestershire, England.
  • 1878 - Starts to learn playing the piano at the age of 5. Displays early signs of great musical talent.
  • 1878 - Composes his first musical piece te same year he began learning the piano.
  • 1880 - Begins taking a college correspondence course in music from Edinburgh University and passes associated exams.
  • 1888 - Organizes his first concert at his school hall.
  • 1890 - Enrolls at the Royal College of Music, London.
  • 1899 - Obtains a Doctor of Music at Cambridge.
  • 1910 - Fantsia on a Theme is featured at two of the largest and most prestigious festivals at the time.
  • 1914 - Volunteers for military service on the outbreak of the first world war despite being 42 years old.
  • 1922 - Produces A Pastoral Symphony, his first major composition since his war service.
  • 1943 - Releases his fith symphony at the Proms.
  • 1958 - Releases his ninth symphony and then passes away on 26 August.
